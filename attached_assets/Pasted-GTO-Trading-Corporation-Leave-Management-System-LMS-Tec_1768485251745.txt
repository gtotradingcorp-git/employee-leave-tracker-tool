GTO Trading Corporation
Leave Management System (LMS)

Technical Documentation

1. Overview

The GTO Trading Corporation Leave Management System (LMS) is a centralized, web-based application designed to streamline employee leave filing, approval, monitoring, and reporting across all departments. The system primarily supports operational teams (Logistics, Frontline, Warehouse) while remaining fully accessible to all departments and management levels.

The LMS ensures accuracy, transparency, and efficiency in leave management for approximately 200 employees, from rank-and-file to executive-level positions.

2. Objectives

Automate employee leave filing and approval workflows

Maintain a centralized and auditable employee leave database

Enable real-time reporting for HR, IT & Digital Transformation, and Top Management

Enforce corporate email registration security

Improve operational efficiency and managerial oversight

14. Leave Credits & Pay Policy Enforcement
14.1 Paid Time Off (PTO) / Leave Credits Allocation

Each employee is entitled to:

Five (5) Paid Time Off (PTO) / Leave Credits per applicable period (as defined by company policy).

These leave credits apply to all applicable leave types unless otherwise specified by HR policy.

14.2 Leave Credit Consumption Rules

The system shall automatically:

Track used leave credits per employee

Deduct approved leave days from the available 5 PTO credits

Leave credit balance is updated in real time after each approved leave.

14.3 Leave Without Pay (LWOP) Handling

Once an employee exceeds the allotted 5 PTO / Leave Credits, any succeeding leave request shall be:

Automatically tagged as LWOP (Leave Without Pay) by the system

LWOP requests:

Still require approval from the employeeâ€™s respective Department Manager

Do not deduct from PTO credits

Are clearly labeled as LWOP in:

Employee leave history

Manager approval screens

HR and payroll-related reports

Top Management summaries

14.4 Manager Approval for LWOP

Managers are explicitly notified when a leave request:

Exceeds the remaining PTO credits

Is classified as LWOP

Managers may:

Approve LWOP (employee may take leave without pay)

Reject LWOP (leave will not be granted)

Manager remarks are required for LWOP approvals for audit and reporting purposes.

14.5 System Validations & Alerts

The system shall provide the following automated validations:

Warning message to employee when:

PTO balance reaches zero

Leave request will be tagged as LWOP

Confirmation prompt before submitting an LWOP request

Visual indicators (icons or labels) for LWOP requests across dashboards

14.6 Reporting & Payroll Integration Support

Reports generated for HR, Accounting, and Top Management shall include:

PTO usage per employee

Remaining leave credit balance

Total LWOP days taken

Department-level LWOP trends

Payroll-impact-ready LWOP summaries

This ensures:

Accurate payroll computation

Transparent leave governance

Operational accountability

14.7 Audit & Compliance Logging

All PTO deductions and LWOP classifications are:

System-logged

Timestamped

Linked to approval authority

Historical data is retained for audit, compliance, and HR review purposes.
